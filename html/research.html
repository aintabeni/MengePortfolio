My Research

My research focuses on improving the prediction and management of water resources through advanced modeling, data science integration, and decision support tools. I work across multiple scales — from local watersheds to regional river basins — to address contemporary challenges in hydrology and water quality.

Current Research Projects

Scalable Decision Support Tool (s-DST)

I lead a project funded by USGS to develop a scalable decision support tool for equitable water resources allocation and management. This tool helps stakeholders make data-driven decisions for sustainable water use.

Climate Resiliency Center

I serve as Principal Investigator for the Climate Resiliency Center, funded by the DOE. We build adaptive capacity in tribal communities along the Missouri River Basin by combining scientific research with community partnerships.

AQUA-CLIME

As a Co-PI on this NSF Track-2 EPSCoR project, I contribute to advancing quality and climate-resilient water management through community partnerships and an enhanced sensor network.

Dryland Cover Crop Research

I collaborate with SDSU to help farmers overcome barriers to cover crop adoption in dryland production systems. This research focuses on improving water use efficiency and soil health.

Research Focus Areas

  • Watershed-scale hydrologic and water quality modeling
  • Stormwater management and green infrastructure
  • Data science integration in water resources
  • Development of decision support tools (DSTs)

Collaboration & Impact

My work brings together field observations, laboratory experiments, and computational modeling to tackle real-world water challenges. I actively collaborate with local communities, governmental agencies, and interdisciplinary research teams to develop solutions that are practical, scalable, and equitable.
